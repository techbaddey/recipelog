Stimulating Drinks

How to make tea

Method:

  1. Heat the teapot by pouring boiling water into it, and leaving it for a short time.
  2. Empty out the water and put in the tea leaves, allow one teaspoonful per person.
  3. Pour really boiling water on the tea leaves, and cover. Leave to infuse for about 3 minutes. The water changes gradually to the colour of the tea leaves and takes its flavour.
  4. Use a strainer and strain the tea into a clean tea cup.
  5. Add sugar and milk to taste.

Note: Tea, when it is infused for a long time, affects digestion. The tannin in the tea leaves is extracted into the liquid tea. This makes the tea bitter, and it has a hardening effect on meat or other protein.

How to make coffee

A hug or a coffee percolator may be used if available.

Method:

  1. Heat the jug as the teapot was heated.
  2. Allow two heaped teaspoonfuls of coffee per person.
  3. Pour boiling water on to the coffee grains and leave for some time.
  4. Add a pinch of salt to the coffee if desired, some people notice and like the little difference it makes.
  5. The coffee gives a better flavour if it is heated again for about two to three minutes before it is served.
  6. Strain into a coffee pot and serve either black or white.

Black coffee is coffee served without milk. The strength of the coffee depends upon the individual. White coffee varies from half milk to half coffee, to 3 parts of milk to 1 part of coffee depending on the person's taste. Hot milk is served and poured into the coffee cup at the same time with the coffee, add sugar to taste.

Refreshing Drinks

Lemonade

1 large lemon
28g sugar
284ml boiling water

Method:

  1. Peel the rind very thinly.
  2. Cut lemon in thin slices.
  3. Put the rind and slices of lemon and the sugar in a jug, and pour boiling water on them.
  4. Cover and leave to cool.
  5. Strain.

Orange Drink

1 orange
284ml cold water
Sugar to taste

First Method:

  1. Squeeze out juice.
  2. Add sugar and water.

Second Method:

  1. Peel the orange very thinly.
  2. Pour 284ml of boiling water on the thin rind, and cover.
  3. Leave until cool, by which time the flavour of the peel will be imparted to the water.
  4. Add the flavoured water to the orange juice.
  5. Sweeten to taste.

Plantain Drink

Method:

  1. Choose very ripe plantain.
  2. Cut into slices - thin rounds preferably.
  3. Pour cold water on the slice, and leave covered for a day or two.
  4. Strain. Drink as it is. More sugar need not to be added, as the ripe plantain will have sweetened it sufficiently. Do not leave it for too long to ferment strongly.

Velvet Tamarind Drink

Method:

  1. Shell and soak the seeds in water overnight to soften.
  2. Stir well and strain the liquid, and add sugar to sweeten.
  3. Stir before serving, as a paste settles.

Grapefruit Drink

Method:

  1. Cut the fruit across into two.
  2. Squeeze out the juice. Add water. Sweeten with sugar.
  3. Chill if wanted, and serve.

To make a drink that will last longer, add more sugar and boil, but it must be remembered that Vitamin C will be sacrificed by this method.
